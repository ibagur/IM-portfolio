Ebola DRC 2026 · Preventive Risk MVP

Partner Guide: what the maps show & how to read them

A plain-language reference to the four risk products and the dashboard hover panel. Every value is a prioritisation ranking to guide surveillance — not a prediction or a probability of infection.

How it works — a graph approach

The MVP represents the country as a graph of health zones and the movement between them, then spreads risk along that network:

  • Nodes — the 519 health zones, indexed by canonical nom.
  • Node features — static variables (population, poverty, health-service access…) from build/long/*.csv.
  • Node signals over time — daily INSP case & Point-of-Entry metrics from build/long/insp_sitrep__*.csv.
  • Edges — mobility, displacement and travel-time relationships between zones, from the matrix CSVs.

11Scripts at a glance

What actually runs in the pipeline — the data each step uses, the method, and the operational question it answers.

#ProductData it usesMethodQuestion it answers
1Zone vulnerability WorldPop population density; GDP per capita (inverted); CCVI socio-economic deprivation & inequality; FAO urban fraction; GRID3 healthsite density (inverted). Refugee-site presence as a side flag. Six features put on a common scale (z-score), equal-weight averaged, converted to a 0–1 within-country percentile and split into five tiers. K-Means (k=5) groups zones into archetypes. "If Ebola reaches this zone, how intrinsically vulnerable is it — population, poverty, health-service access?"
2PoE (border) risk INSP daily Point-of-Entry counts (passengers passed / screened / refused); Imperial College cross-border passenger volumes. Per screening point: unscreened travellers/day = passed − screened (volume × screening leakage), averaged over the latest window. Busy crossings with no screening data are flagged unmonitored. "Which border crossings have the highest screening-gap exposure, and which busy crossings are unmonitored?"
3Importation risk Flowminder mobility (outflow) + gravity-model fallback for uncovered zones + recent IOM-DTM displacement (monthly); INSP confirmed cases as seeds. A movement graph between all 519 zones; Personalised PageRank (α = 0.85) spreads risk outward from zones with a confirmed case in the last 7 days. 100 bootstrap runs give a confidence band. "Given where cases are active now, which currently unaffected zones are most network-close to the outbreak?"
4Transmission corridors The same movement graph as #3, plus the top-20 importation-risk destinations and zone centroids. Shortest "effective-distance" paths (high-probability movement = short) from each active-case zone to the high-risk destinations, capped at 4 hops, drawn as map lines. "Which plausible movement routes explain why those zones are at risk?"
5Dashboard All four outputs above + the 519-zone map. A single self-contained interactive map (Leaflet) with toggleable layers, hover details and a disclaimer. "One combined operational view for coordinators."
Where this differs from the original plan (deliberate, data-driven)
  • Vulnerability dropped a "nearest-facility travel time" feature (518 of 519 zones already contain a facility, so it carried no signal) and moved refugee-site presence to a flag rather than a scored feature. The composite uses six clean features.
  • PoE risk is computed from the INSP data alone (volume and screening from the same records), because the Imperial volume and INSP screening datasets overlap on only two zones. Imperial volumes are kept as context and to flag unmonitored crossings.
  • Importation risk uses the Flowminder outflow matrix (rows = where people leave from) and the monthly displacement matrix (the weekly file is too sparse).

All outputs are deterministic (fixed random seed) and key on the canonical zone name nom, so they slot directly into the 519-zone map.

12Reading the dashboard tooltip

Hovering a health zone fills the left-hand panel. Below is a worked example (Mambasa) and a line-by-line guide.

Mambasa

Vulnerability: 0.934 (Very High)
Archetype: high_inequality|low_gdp_pc
Raised by: high_inequality, low_gdp_pc, high_deprivation
Lowered by: low_urban_fraction, low_pop_density
Importation risk: 23 / 100 (rank #44)
Confidence: ±20% (stable)
Graph source: flowminder
On the watchlist by network proximity to active-case zones via observed mobility / displacement / travel-time fallback.
LineWhat it tells youHow to read it
Zone name
Mambasa		 The health zone under the cursor. A red ACTIVE CASE SEED tag appears instead of a rank if the zone is currently reporting confirmed cases.
Vulnerability
0.934 (Very High)		 How vulnerable the zone is if cases arrive — a 0–1 ranking against all 519 zones, plus its tier. Higher = more vulnerable (0.934 ≈ top 7% nationally). A consequence / readiness score, not where the outbreak is moving.
Archetype
high_inequality|low_gdp_pc		 The "type" of zone (from clustering) — its two strongest traits. A planning label for grouping similar zones. Not a number to rank on.
Raised by The three factors pushing this zone's vulnerability up. Read literally: low_gdp_pc = low income, low_healthsite_density = poor health-service access. Explains why the score is high.
Lowered by The factors pulling the score down — where the zone is below the national average (protective terms). Two zones with the same "Raised by" can score very differently because of this line. A strong entry (e.g. low_inequality) is often what makes an otherwise-average zone score Very Low. Blank for the most vulnerable zones.
Importation risk
23 / 100 (rank #44)		 An easy-to-compare 0–100 index of how network-close the zone is to active cases, with its watchlist rank and a colour bar. Higher number / fuller bar = higher priority. Scaled so the top watchlist zone = 100; bar colour matches the map. rank #44 = 43 zones rank above it. A relative ranking, not a probability.
Confidence
±20% (stable)		 How sensitive the index is to data uncertainty (from 100 bootstrap runs). Smaller % = more reliable: stable <25% moderate 25–50% uncertain >50%. An "uncertain" zone may jump between refreshes.
Graph source
flowminder		 Whether the zone's movement links come from observed data or a model. flowminder = observed mobility (higher confidence); gravity_fallback = estimated from population & travel time (lower confidence — interpret cautiously).
Narrative line A plain-language summary of why the zone appears. For active-case zones this instead states the zone is currently reporting cases (a diffusion seed).
One-sentence brief for partners: the colour shows where the outbreak is most likely to reach next (importation risk); the vulnerability score shows how hard it would be to respond there if it does; the lines show the movement routes that connect them. All values are prioritisation rankings to guide surveillance — not predictions or probabilities.
