2. Data Workflow

The diagram below shows how raw data sources are transformed into the dashboard outputs. Zone name harmonisation is applied to every source before joining — the DRC health zone names are inconsistently spelled across datasets and must be reconciled to the canonical shapefile name.

Zone name harmonisation is a prerequisite for every join in the pipeline. The DRC health zone names differ across datasets — e.g. Mongbwalu in some sources maps to Mongbalu in the shapefile. A lookup table of observed-to-canonical name mappings ensures all sources join correctly to the 519-zone geometry. Two zones (Bili, Lubunga) exist in two provinces and require a province suffix to disambiguate.

3. Zone Classification Criteria

Each of the 519 DRC health zones is assigned a MoH response category based on epidemiological data. Categories A and B are recalculated automatically each time new SitRep data is received. Category C is the only manual component.

Category A — Active transmission zone

Criterion: the zone has at least one confirmed case recorded at its most recent SitRep reporting date.

Source: INSP SitRep — per-zone cumulative confirmed cases (daily series).

Cat A zones are the primary risk areas driving the response. They are the origin zones for the importation risk and mobility layers.

Category B — Alert zone

Either of the following qualifies a zone as Cat B (zone must not already be Cat A):

B — Adjacent (limitrophe): the zone centroid lies within 100 m of any Cat A zone polygon boundary. A 100 m tolerance is used because the health zone shapefile contains micro-gaps between adjacent polygons; a strict boundary-touch test misses genuine neighbours.
B — Contacts: the zone has at least one traced contact of a confirmed case at its most recent SitRep date.
Source: INSP SitRep — per-zone cumulative traced contacts.
Category C — Preparedness zone

Criterion: zone is listed in the national MoH Ebola response plan as requiring preparedness activities (commercial axes, provincial interconnection corridors).

This list is manually curated and cannot be derived from SitRep data. It is maintained as a static input to the pipeline.

A zone previously classified as B that no longer meets either B criterion is retained as C rather than dropped entirely.

Uncategorised zones

Zones not meeting any criterion are rendered in neutral grey on the map. They receive no MoH response package.

The vast majority of the 519 national health zones fall into this group — the response is geographically concentrated in Ituri, Nord-Kivu, Haut-Uele and Tshopo provinces.

Category C is a curated input, not a computed output. Any ArcGIS replication must maintain Cat C as a static attribute on the zones layer. It cannot be recalculated from SitRep data alone — the MoH plan designation must be preserved and updated manually.

Category precedence

The classification is strictly hierarchical: if a zone qualifies for Cat A, it is Cat A regardless of adjacency or contacts. Cat B is assigned only to zones that do not qualify as Cat A. Cat C is assigned only to zones that qualify for neither A nor B and appear on the MoH plan list.

Update frequency

The dashboard is designed to be refreshed with each new SitRep release. Cat A and Cat B are recalculated from the latest data. Cat C requires manual review when new SitRep data shows zones crossing category boundaries.
